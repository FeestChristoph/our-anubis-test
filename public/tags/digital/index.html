Platforms for Good

In 2017, at the World Economic Forum, Bill Gates and a group of leading philanthropists announced the launch of Co-Impact, a platform hub aimed at working collaboratively to drive social change at scale in three fields—health, education, and economic opportunity. This initiative combines platforms’ design with the need to address societal grand challenges, as embodied by the UN’s sustainable development goals. Is Co-impact an isolated example, or is it part of a wider phenomenon?

This is a first - do it open and digital

It is one week since Pfizer & BioNTech announced that their first interim analysis “indicates a [COVID-19] vaccine efficacy rate above 90%, at seven days after the second dose.” Similar announcements will arrive over the next few weeks for other vaccine candidates currently in phase 3 clinical trials. With regulatory approvals expedited, it is plausible that by Spring 2021, several vaccines are on the market and deployed. Companies and governments are preparing logistics and drawing up distribution schemes at speed for a vaccination campaign that will take years and involve a number of different vaccines.

Unmet needs

Advanced therapies cure diseases instead of treating symptoms. Machine learning will help reach more patients sooner. The term advanced therapies is often used to describe interventions in the vanguard of biomedicine: Based on cells, genes or tissue engineering these ‘living drugs’ can treat chronic conditions and often restore the functionality of diseased tissue. Their first wave has arrived in the clinic, most notably CAR-T cells battling blood cancer and gene therapies for rare diseases such as muscular dystrophy.
